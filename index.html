Undergraduate Student in Applied Math and Optics

E-mail: dtorresb@arizona.edu

I am an undergraduate at the University of Arizona in the applied math program with a minor in optics. I am currently an undergradute researcher at the Steward Observatory Solar Lab working in radio telescope manufacturing and a TIMESTEP intern at the University of Arizona Space Astrophysics Lab working in polarization characterization. I am also involved at the Student Optics Chapter (SOCk) in outreach events in optics.

Projects

Welcome to my projects portfolio! Here, you’ll find a collection of the work I’ve been passionate about throughout my academic and professional journey. These projects span across various domains, including optics, applied mathematics, and quantum science, reflecting my commitment to pushing the boundaries of innovation and research. Each project showcases a unique challenge I’ve tackled, whether it’s developing advanced metrology systems using LiDAR technology, designing guided-mode resonance devices for quantum applications, or creating sophisticated software tools for data analysis and modeling. My work is driven by a deep curiosity and a desire to solve complex problems, always aiming to contribute meaningful advancements to the field. Feel free to explore these projects, which highlight my technical expertise, problem-solving abilities, and dedication to research. Whether through hands-on experimentation or detailed simulations, these experiences have honed my skills and prepared me for future challenges in graduate studies and beyond.
